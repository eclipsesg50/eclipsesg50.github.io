Vincent's review

The first day of the try-out, we were told to build a bridge with ice-cream sticks which is at least 5 cm wide and 30 cm long. That activity allowed me to interact with my new mates and find out their strengths and weaknesses. It didn’t take me long to get that information. We were given 30 minutes to build finish the bridge. While we were brainstorming on what designs we want and I pointed out the possibilities of errors that might cause the structure to be weak. Some of those include, the joints held by hot glue, between 2 ice-cream sticks. The bridge was able to hold a weight of 5 hammers, which is awesome. From that activity I learn some of the skills in using a hot glue, which is to immediately glue after the hot glue is being applied to surface. We were then asked to design the paper is such that the paper can fly. 2 designs for accuracy and 1design for distance. Obviously, many students will make a paper plane, but I wanted to make something different. So, I fold the paper into a business card shaped and started throwing it. I personally have a skill in card throwing so it is easy for me to throw far and accurate. We test out our designs and my design flew far as expected. We were then taught html. It is used for recording the progress when we make the launcher. We were then taught some basics of Arduino programming and how to operate different kinds of motor. Personally, I know how to use basic AutoCAD and how to write basic code for Arduino. The process of building the launcher was long so I won’t go into the details, but it was worth it when we tested our launcher. The launcher managed to launch the paper plane for around 4 meters. It is a great achievement but at the same time It can also be improved At the end of the 3-days try-outs, I feel that Engineering Academy is fun, but I am also aware that the actual program isn’t as simple as what the instructors guide and show us. I am looking forward to joining the Engineering Academy. I always believed a saying where the only limit to human potential is imagination.
